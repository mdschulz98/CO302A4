To be fair, I'm supposed to be one of the good guys, and I am, but it's worth the lesson to make sure you're always paying attention to link addresses. Chrome usually shows them in the bottom left corner of your browser when you hover over a link. To make sure the links you're clicking are actually going to the site you think they are, it's important to check these. As you can see, the link for the real/fake buttons showed "http://www.hackers-website-to-steal-your-data.com", and that's obviously not a link you want to click. In a real setting, it is unlikely to be this obvious, but looking for things like common company names with numbers in place of some letters, random strings of letters, and domains (.something) that do not match the current one (ie. you're on natsci.colostate.edu and the link shows www.darpa.mil) will help you stay safe.
