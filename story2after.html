No matter how real a webpage may look, one dead giveaway that it's fake is its URL. Shown at the top of most browsers, the URL basically identifies where you are on the internet. If it does not match where you thought you were - be extremely careful, leave the page, and report the site if you can. In our example, the URL should involve "Bank Of America" or "BoA", etc, but shows "sclgchl1.eu.pn/index3.html" instead. Tips for noticing fake URL's are similar to detecting fake emails and links: mismatched domain names, numbers instead of letters, and random strings of letters (OpenDNS).
