After The Story

In the story, there were choices about list, of, things, in, story. In this webtext I will explain each of the choices and attempt to demystify computer security, also called cybersecurity. Computer security is a branch of computer science dealing with the safety of information on and traveling between computers. It is both extremely important and extremely hard to implement and understand, and I will to translate the most relevant parts of it into a clear and accessible form. The most important part of computer security for everyone to understand is the importance of antivirus software, and the examination of several agreements on this importance follows. The bottom line of elementary computer security is that everyone should utilize antivirus software and apply basic internet security practices when interacting with computers.

Importance and Difficulty

Cybersecurity evolves and changes as we grow to understand both computers and security better. Additionally and unfortunately, there are two sides to to every coin: for every security-minded lawful programmer there are many hackers trying to steal your data. Combining the relative novelty of this topic, the aforementioned ratio, and the difficulty of securing online entities, I present it is not a stretch and may even be an understatment to call cybersecurity extremely difficult. One of the reasons cybersecurity is so tough is demonstrated by Wallden and Kashefi in their article "Cyber Security in the Quantum Era", where the implications of quantum computing on current security meaures is discussed and analyzed. They find that in quantum computing the field is going to have to advance further to handle computing that is not possible currently. For instance, RSA is an encryption mechnanism used by many social media sites, banks, and more to make sure your data isn't readable to anyone but you, and "it would be the demise of RSA should quantum computing happen..." (Numberphile2).

Closing

Through the story and this webtext the basics of computer security and advocacy of antivirus software have been discussed and clarified. The field of computer security has much, much more depth, and so does its counterpart hacking. This means there are innumerably more security topics to be worried about, however, that is the job of cybersecurity professionals and trained, experienced programmers - not the average internet user. Fortuitously, average users can abstract the responsibility of complicated cybersecurity to the programmers, but the responsiblity of using software and the internet safely is immutably the user's. The knowledge of basic cybersecurity principles (review each mention here) and user of and antivirus is ordinarily enough to fulfill that duty. I encourage all internet users to make sure they are aware of and understand basic internet safety practices and to make use of antivirus software.
