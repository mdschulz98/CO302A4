There are 3 key things that let you know this email is fake, and all 3 of them boil down to a lack of grammar or spelling. First, we notice there's no period after "temporarily", which is uncharacteristic for trustworthy emails. Second, "informations" is written where "information" should go. Third and lastly, "you are currently made disabled of" is terrible grammar, and is almost a surefire sign that this email is fake and the links are very likely dangerous. To detect fake emails, make sure to look for poor grammar, poor spelling, and anything that looks out of place. Most companies scrutinize their online appearance, which creates correct syntax and uniformity - something scam emails tend to ignore.
